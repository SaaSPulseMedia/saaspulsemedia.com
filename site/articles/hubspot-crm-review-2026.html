HubSpot CRM Review 2026: Still the Best Free CRM?

HubSpot's free CRM has been the default choice for SMBs for years. But is it still the right pick in 2026, or have competitors caught up?

✍ SaaS Pulse Media 📅 28 May 2026 ⏱ 2 min read

HubSpot CRM in 2026: The Honest Take

HubSpot has spent the last decade becoming the de-facto CRM for small and medium businesses. Its free tier is genuinely generous — unlimited contacts, deal tracking, email integration, and a solid mobile app, all at no cost.

But in 2026, the landscape has shifted. Competitors have closed the feature gap, and HubSpot's paid tiers have become noticeably more expensive. So is it still the right choice?

What HubSpot Does Well

The free tier is legitimately powerful. Unlike many 'free' CRMs that restrict you to 10 contacts, HubSpot gives you unlimited contacts, unlimited deals, and a full email integration from day one. For a bootstrapped business just getting started with CRM, it's hard to beat.

The UI is exceptionally well-designed. Sales teams with no CRM experience can get up to speed in hours. The pipeline view is intuitive, the contact timeline is clean, and the mobile app is one of the best in class.

The ecosystem is unmatched. HubSpot's native integrations with over 1,000 tools, plus its Marketing Hub and Service Hub, mean you can run your entire customer lifecycle in one platform if you choose to.

Where HubSpot Falls Short

Paid tier pricing is aggressive. Moving from the free tier to Professional unlocks features like sequences and advanced reporting — but at $450/month for just 5 seats, it's a significant jump.

Reporting is weaker than Salesforce. If you need complex, customised dashboards pulling from multiple data sources, HubSpot's reporting tools still lag behind Salesforce's.

Customisation has limits. Enterprise teams with complex workflows often hit the ceiling of what HubSpot allows without significant workarounds.

Should You Use HubSpot in 2026?

Yes, if: You're a small to medium business looking for an easy-to-adopt CRM with a solid free tier and room to grow.

Consider alternatives if: You're an enterprise with complex reporting needs (Salesforce), a startup on a tight budget who needs sequences from day one (Pipedrive), or a team wanting deeper automation (ActiveCampaign).

Verdict: 8.5/10

HubSpot remains the best starting point for most SMBs. The free tier is genuinely the most generous in the market, the UI is excellent, and the pathway to Marketing Hub integration is compelling. Just go in with eyes open about the cost cliff when you need to upgrade.
